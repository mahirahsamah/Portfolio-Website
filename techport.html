Technical Portfolio

Co-Project Manager of StudyBuddy

My co-project manager and I came up with our app idea "StudyBuddy" at a hackathon. It was a learning-oriented project under the Aggie Coding Club, where we used Java for front-end UI design and Python/Google Cloud API for the back-end functionalities. This app allows students to take pictures of their hand-written notes, and it identifies only the highlighted words in that picture and make a flashcard of each word for the student via a web dictionary search. We worked with a team of ten students, and it was split into two, one sub-team worked on the front-end and the other on the back-end.

Supplemental Instruction (SI) Leader

I taught Engineering Mechanics to freshmen using self-made worksheets and teaching strategies. Having taken this class as a freshman, I would show my students how to effectively study, practice, and take notes to maximize their physics potential. I conducted sessions over Zoom three days a week, with an average of 15 students every session. SI leaders were required to make worksheets for every session, which put a sense of responsibility alongside academics on my shoulders. It was difficult to navigate both commitments at once, but I eventually found a balance and started enjoying my sessions. I would get to use different collaboration devices during my sessions to ensure that students were being active, and it always filled me with joy when they thanked me at the end of the sessions for helping them.
